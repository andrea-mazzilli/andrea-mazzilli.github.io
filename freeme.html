Case Study / Product UX

FreeMe: Reducing Cognitive Load for Informal Caregivers

Operating within a conceptual 3-month Agile design sprint, I designed FreeMe, a mobile application focused on managing the personal leisure time of informal caregivers. By implementing a data-driven, 3-cycle iteration engine, we transformed an assumption-based prototype into an optimized, user-validated solution, pivoting the architecture to address a 66% baseline task success rate. The result is a frictionless, accessible interface tailored to users experiencing high mental fatigue.

Project Hero & Recruiter Snapshot

Identifying an Unaddressed Market Gap

Competitor analysis revealed a significant gap in the market: existing applications for informal caregivers are heavily saturated with social support and forum features. None actively addressed the caregiver's personal leisure time or psychological need to disconnect.

Caregivers suffer from high decision fatigue and stress. They struggle to optimize what little free time they have. The product strategy became clear: build a tool that delegates decision-making and minimizes cognitive load.

"Since I have to make decisions everyday and do things for me and others, I like having someone that gives me an idea and that doesn't make me think about what to do." — Target User Feedback

Problem Statement

From Qualitative Data to Core Architecture

Primary research consisted of semi-structured interviews synthesized using Grounded Theory. Information architecture was subsequently validated via hybrid card sorting.

Key Strategic Insights:

Decision Delegation

The research drove the creation of our core feature, "Lasciati ispirare" (Let yourself be inspired). Instead of browsing catalogs, users input their available time and mood, and the system curates the activity.

Modular Content

Users required easily interruptible activities. The content architecture was split into digestible segments: Relaxation, Movement, and Video Courses.

Research & Evidence

Data-Driven UI Pivots

A mid-fidelity prototype underwent a cognitive walkthrough, followed by a rigorous think-aloud usability test involving 11 core tasks. The baseline metrics established a 66% task success rate, 25% partial success, and 9% failure. We utilized the failure points to drive direct architectural pivots.

3-Cycle Iteration Engine

Before/After Comparison

Pivot 1: Architectural Consolidation (Fixing Task 9 Failure)

The Problem: Users failed to differentiate between the "Corsi iniziati" (Begun courses) and "Riprendi da dove hai lasciato" (Start back where you left off) hubs, causing navigation errors.

The Fix: Merged both streams into a single, unified "In corso" (In progress) page. The profile page was made non-scrollable to keep all features above the fold.

[PLACEHOLDER: AFTER - Unified In Corso Page]

Pivot 2: Interaction Redesign (Fixing Task 11 Failure)

The Problem: Users failed to switch between activities and courses because a crucial UI toggle was placed at the top of the screen, outside their primary reading focus.

The Fix: The toggle was enlarged, renamed for clarity, and anchored to the bottom of the screen, aligning with established CTA interaction patterns where the user's attention was naturally focused.

Pivot 3: Friction Reduction (Fixing Task 1 Failure)

The Problem: Users failed to save courses because they did not recognize the standalone bookmark and share icons.

The Fix: Appended explicit text labels ("Salva", "Condividi") to all utility icons, prioritizing clarity over minimalism.

Final System & Product Logic

Designing for Calm and Retention

Accessible Visual Identity

The UI utilizes rounded shapes and a desaturated green/blue palette (#405D60, #8BF3AF) designed to evoke calm and security. All color combinations were validated for color blindness compatibility using Coolors.

Systemic Gamification

To drive retention without inducing stress, we implemented Yu-kai Chou's Octalysis Framework. The system utilizes non-punitive progression loops, including soft badges and flexible progress bars, mapped to self-realization metrics.

AI-Accelerated Workflow

Maintained high velocity by leveraging tools like NotebookLM, ChatGPT, and Hyperwrite for rapid mock-data generation, copywriting, and ideation framing, ensuring the focus remained strictly on structural UX decisions.

Retrospective & Constraints

Tradeoffs and Ethical Decision Making

Operating within a conceptual environment meant a lack of live market metrics or post-launch analytics. I optimized heavily for what I could measure: qualitative user satisfaction and task completion rates.

A critical product tradeoff involved data ethics. Early ideation included an "Emotional Profiling" feature intended to dynamically curate content based on the user's daily mental fatigue. I actively scoped this feature out of the MVP to protect the sensitive emotional data of a highly vulnerable user base, prioritizing user privacy over algorithmic personalization.
